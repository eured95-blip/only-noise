01  ·  Thesis

Psychedelic phenomenology is where two disciplines meet.

It is the careful study of what an experience is actually like — coded, compared across many people, and never flattened into a checklist or discarded as noise. Phenomenology is the study of experience from the inside: not the receptor or the behaviour, but the way a situation actually shows up for the person living it. For a science of the mind, that first-person account is not decoration. It is data.

A single dose can bring serenity in one session and terror in the next, and that variability is not random. It is shaped by mindset and environment — set and setting — as much as by pharmacology. How context steers the experience is the open problem this work is built around. only_noise is small and slow by choice, and part of the Albert Hofmann Institute.

03  ·  The work

How an experience is read.

The work turns first-person psychedelic narratives into structured, analysable data — without flattening the detail that makes them worth studying.

Each account is read feature by feature against a defined codebook. Higher-order constructs are reconstructed from the confirmed features, never assigned directly; where the text is ambiguous the code is left unassigned — under-reading is preferred to over-reading. VEIL is the field half of the work; behind it sits a second, more involved layer that reads written reports at larger scale against the same scheme. A human remains the final interpreter.

04  ·  Context

Where the experience sits.

Set and setting are usually described at the scale of the room — mood, company, surroundings. But the room sits inside a legal system, an economy, and a set of cultural stories, and those larger conditions shape an experience as surely as the near ones do. Treating context as a variable means taking that wider frame seriously rather than stopping at the walls.

Criminalisation as setting

Where a substance is illegal, use is shaped by secrecy, by uncertain dose and purity, and by the absence of aftercare, against a background of legal risk. Each belongs to the setting, and each can enter the phenomenology — as caution held in the body, as isolation, as the tenor of a difficult passage. A report written under prohibition describes an experience that prohibition helped shape.

The research is not neutral ground

The same conditions constrain what can be studied. For decades controlled research was heavily restricted, and much of what survives about non-clinical use is first-person report precisely because other channels were closed. What can be funded, published, and safely reported is shaped by policy; naturalistic corpora are in part an artefact of that history.

Two public stories

These substances tend to be narrated in cycles — moral panic, then therapeutic promise. Neither describes the phenomenon well: the first dismisses the account, the second polishes it for use. Under-coding, tracking difficulty as its own measure, and saying only what the text supports are, in part, a correction to both.

Attention & cognitive liberty

The current wave of interest is also a market — investment, intellectual property, and media attention shape which experiences count as legitimate and which are passed over. Cognitive liberty — that a person's inner states are their own, and that reliable knowledge about them is worth protecting — is the position this work operates from.

Between prohibition, which produces ignorance, and promotion, which produces a flattened story, there is a third option: careful, structured, first-person observation — held to a method, honest about its limits. That is what structured psychonautics names here.

06  ·  Field

VEIL — the instrument you can hold.

Most trip reports are written days later, tidied into a story. VEIL is a small, private instrument for catching the account closer to the source. On a phone or tablet, it listens as someone tells their experience, then names the phenomenology their own words already carry — and asks them to confirm or strike every reading. It is not a diagnosis and never advice. Everything happens on the device; nothing leaves it unless the teller chooses to share.

Now in testing  ·  festivals & online

This season VEIL is being tested in two places: at festivals — a quiet field station on a single tablet, consent logged first — and online, open to anyone curious enough to walk through the tent. Every session a tester chooses to share makes the coding sharper. If you have carried an experience and want to help the instrument read it better, you are exactly who this is for.
