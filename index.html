01  ·  Thesis

Psychedelic phenomenology is where two disciplines meet.

It is the careful study of what an experience is actually like — coded, compared across many people, and never flattened into a checklist or discarded as noise. Phenomenology is the study of experience from the inside: not the receptor or the behaviour, but the way a situation actually shows up for the person living it. For a science of the mind, that first-person account is not decoration. It is data.

A single dose can bring serenity in one session and terror in the next, and that variability is not random. It is shaped by mindset and environment — set and setting — as much as by pharmacology. How context steers the experience is the open problem this work is built around. only_noise is small and slow by choice, and part of the Albert Hofmann Institute.

03  ·  The work

How an experience is read.

The work turns first-person psychedelic narratives into structured, analysable data — without flattening the detail that makes them worth studying.

Each account is read in phases and coded feature by feature against a defined codebook, with a verbatim quote required for anything marked present. Higher-order constructs are reconstructed from the confirmed features, never assigned directly; where the text is ambiguous, the code is left unassigned. A human remains the final interpreter.

05  ·  Field

VEIL — the instrument you can hold.

Most trip reports are written days later, tidied into a story. VEIL is a small, private instrument for catching the account closer to the source. On a phone or tablet, it listens as someone tells their experience, then names the phenomenology their own words already carry — and asks them to confirm or strike every reading. It is not a diagnosis and never advice. Everything happens on the device; nothing leaves it unless the teller chooses to share.

Now in testing  ·  festivals & online

This season VEIL is being tested in two places: at festivals — a quiet field station on a single tablet, consent logged first — and online, open to anyone curious enough to walk through the tent. Every session a tester chooses to share makes the coding sharper. If you have carried an experience and want to help the instrument read it better, you are exactly who this is for.
