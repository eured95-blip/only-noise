01  ·  VEIL

VEIL — the instrument you can hold.

Most trip reports are written days later, tidied into a story. VEIL is a small, private instrument for catching the account closer to the source. On a phone or tablet, it listens as someone tells their experience, then names the phenomenology their own words already carry — and asks them to confirm or strike every reading. It is not a diagnosis and never advice. Everything happens on the device; nothing leaves it unless the teller chooses to share.

Now in testing  ·  festivals & online

This season VEIL is being tested in two places: at festivals — a quiet field station on a single tablet, consent logged first — and online, open to anyone curious enough to walk through the tent. Every session a tester chooses to share makes the coding sharper. If you have carried an experience and want to help the instrument read it better, you are exactly who this is for.

04  ·  Reading

A map of the field.

Eleven annotated readings — the phenomenological tradition, psychedelic phenomenology proper, the set-and-setting question, and the computational methods this work draws on.

01
Varela (1996)
Neurophenomenology: a methodological remedy for the hard problem.
Journal of Consciousness Studies

The charter for treating rigorous first-person description and third-person neuroscience as one enterprise, each disciplining the other rather than working alone.
02
Preller & Vollenweider (2018)
Phenomenology, structure, and dynamics of psychedelic states.
Current Topics in Behavioral Neurosciences

A comprehensive map of the psychedelic state’s dimensions and their links to the standard instruments and their neural correlates — a single-source orientation.
03
Millière (2017)
Looking for the self: drug-induced ego dissolution.
Frontiers in Human Neuroscience

A careful anatomy of ego dissolution, distinguishing its several varieties — essential for one of the state’s most striking and theoretically loaded features.
04
Hartogsohn (2017)
Constructing drug effects: a history of set and setting.
Drug Science, Policy and Law

How the field came to recognise that a drug’s effects are shaped by mindset and environment as much as by pharmacology. It frames the central question here.
05
Carhart-Harris & Friston (2019)
REBUS and the anarchic brain.
Pharmacological Reviews

The influential “relaxed beliefs under psychedelics” model, linking phenomenology, self, and predictive processing into one theoretical backbone.
06
Brouwer et al. (2025)
A qualitative analysis of the mushroom come-up and come-down.
npj Mental Health Research

Evidence that onset tends toward distress and the descent toward relief — a demonstration that phase, not only dose, carries meaning. It informs the phase-aware coding.
07
McGovern et al. (2025)
Deconstructing psychedelic phenomenology: discrete phases.
Brain and Behavior

People spontaneously narrate their experiences in stages, and each stage’s themes map onto set-and-setting factors — a model for the phase-structured approach.
08
Ballentine, Friedman & Bzdok (2022)
Trips and neurotransmitters: patterns across 6,850 experiences.
Science Advances

A landmark showing that natural-language methods can recover receptor-linked structure from thousands of free-form reports at scale.
09
Tagliazucchi (2022)
Language as a window into the altered state of consciousness.
Frontiers in Pharmacology

How both what people say and how they say it can characterise — and help predict — the psychedelic state. It surveys the terrain this pipeline operates in.
10
Bzdok et al. (2024)
Large language models auto-profile conscious awareness changes.
Research Square preprint

A proof of concept for LLM-scale annotation of experiential dimensions across free-form reports — the closest published parallel to the automated coding strand here.
11
Ermakova et al. (2025)
Mapping the phenomenology of intranasal 5-MeO-DMT in psychedelic-naïve adults.
Scientific Reports

A phase-1 trial that used microphenomenology and language analysis to map how a short-acting psychedelic unfolds over time — the clinical cousin of this project’s method.
